Police Complaints and Accountability

Do officers with more complaints face more or fewer consequences?

More Repercussions Less Repercussions Writeup

More Complaints → More Repercussions

This visualization suggests that officers with more complaints accumulate more substantiated cases, indicating increased consequences.

More Complaints → Less Repercussions

This visualization shows that the proportion of substantiated complaints decreases as complaint count increases, suggesting weaker accountability.

Checkpoint Writeup

  • Proposition: Officers with more complaints face more repercussions.
  • Opposing: Officers with more complaints face fewer repercussions.
  • Leaning toward: Less repercussions (based on decreasing substantiation rates).
  • Techniques used: binning, selective metrics (counts vs %), persuasive titles.

Data: ProPublica NYPD Complaints Dataset
